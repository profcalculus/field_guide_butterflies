Identification

Small sapphire. Upper side superficially similar to Red–line Sapphire Iolaus sidus, but dark median line crosses hind wing. Underside has conspicuous black–edged orange–yellow bands crossing underside of both wings. Very similar on both surfaces to Yellow–banded Sapphire Iolaus aphnaeoides and Natal Yellow–banded Sapphire I. diametra natalica. The three butterflies have very different ranges. The underside bands are much wider than those of I. aphnaeoides or I. diametra natalica.

 

Sexes differ; female with wings more rounded, and white edge to forewing upperside blue; area distal to hind wing upperside postdiscal transverse dark stripe white, not blue as in male.

 

Males fly around tall treetops and shrubs.. Female usually found near the host plant.
