Identification

Sparkling red–copper Opal; Upper side shows gold to green iridescence at certain angles. Similar to many other Opals with plain copper uppersides but most are not found in the same areas. Sometimes confused with Mooi River Opal Chrysoritis lycegenes, but larger, red–orange deeper, well–defined rows of discal spots on upperside, both wings. Another species that flies in the same areas is Burnished Opal C. chrysaor, but that species has a curved line of black discal spots on the hind wing. Underside forewing has typical 'Copper' pattern of black spots on a pale ground with dark borders. Underside hind wing is red–brown to greenish–buff crossed by lines of darker brown.

 

Sexes similar; female orange paler, less metallic than male, wings rounder.

 

Colonies, including territorial males, occur in rocky outcrops because the larval host plant only grows among these rocks. Both sexes feed on nectar.

Distribution

Montane Grassland and edges of Afromontane Forest from n KwaZulu–Natal (Utrecht) to Mpumalanga (Wakkerstroom hills and Vaalkop, near Morgenzon); along Drakensberg escarpment to Mariepskop (Limpopo Province), further west in high mountains near Dullstroom, Lydenburg and Machadodorp (Mpumalanga).
