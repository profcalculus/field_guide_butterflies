Identification

Similar to Neita Brown N. neita, but slightly smaller; further distinguished by having a dark line around the postdiscal upperside forewing orange–red patch. Upper side superficially resembles the smaller Natal Brown Coenyropsis natalii but the forewing orange–red patch does not completely surround the ocellus.

 

Sexes similar, but female with brighter colouring and wings more rounded.

 

Found at lower altitude in warmer, more wooded country than other Neita species. Slow, fluttering flight around large bushes in grassy Savanna.
