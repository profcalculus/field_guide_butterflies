Identification

The male of this Bitter Acraea is a very conspicuous deep, pure red, making him one of our most noticeable butterflies.

 

Sexually dimorphic; female duller, varying from blackish ochre-brown to brown-red, with white forewing upperside subapical patches. Flight slow and fluttering.

 

Slow, fluttering flight with periods of sailing; females usually found close to the host plant, which is a popular coastal garden subject. Male defends territories at edges of clearings. Both sexes fond of flowers, particularly Clerodendrum glabrum and Deinbollia oblongifolia.
