Identification

Small to medium sized, dull coloured lycaenid. Very similar to Silver–spotted Grey Crudaria leromabut tail always absent; series of forewing postdiscal spots on underside form a line parallel to the outer margin. Similar to Cape Grey Crudaria capensis but has less contrast between underside pale marks and ground colour.

 

Sexes similar; male darker than female, wings more pointed.

 

Flies rapidly over open ground between thorn trees, settling often on low plants or on the ground. Resting posture with wings open and forewings held proud.
